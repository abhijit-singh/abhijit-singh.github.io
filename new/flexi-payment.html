Flexi Payment Module

Primary Requirements
– A payment solution for orders with high transaction amounts, where the payment is to be collected in two or more parts, with each part payment having a certain last date.
Secondary Requirements
– Scheduling payment for a future date.
– Sending periodic payment reminders to the end customer
– Collecting part payments of the same order from multiple participating customers.

My Role

– Uncovering & validating all user requirements in relation to collecting & tracking payments
– Conceptualizing an appropriate solution to satisfy user requirements without disrupting the core payment flows.
– Wireframing/mocking up the concept, validating it with the users and handing it over for development.

Requirement Analysis

– Interacted with customer support specialists to get an overview of the basic requirements.
– Validated the requirements with selected end users and tried to uncover additional requirements & expectations.

Majority Use Case – Collect payment in 2 steps, where the first payment is to be made in a short time span, while the remaining payment could be made in parts over a long period of time, or at once just before the trip was scheduled.
Additional Needs - Send payment reminders before respective cut-off dates, and also an option to schedule periodic reminders. Also, there was a use case to collect payments for the same booking from different customers, and that too in parts, mostly for loosely coupled groups.

Conceptualizing the Solution

After considering and discussing multiple solutions ranging from integrating with 3rd Party EMI payment gateways to revamping the entire payment collection flow, the optimum solution came out as a collective idea, which was to build a plugin for all advanced payment actions and just adding the basic cut-off functionality in the core flow.

I made the information & user flows which went through multiple iterations to eliminate all complexity. They were then plugged into various user scenarios to ensure they work.

Mockups & Protoype

As the information flow was uncomplicated & the final UI was expected to be minimal enough, wireframes were very close to high fidelity mockups. The major challenge was the plugin screen, which gave the user a plethora of options to set up a new payment link. Most options were independent and needed to be discoverable yet unobtrusive. Text dropdowns was a UX pattern that helped considerably to deal with this. The other catch was to identify and take care of all states of the payment and convey the appropriate messaging to not only the admin but also the end user. The flexi-payment option wasn't something the average travel buyer is used to, and educating them of the choices they had was critical to the success of this feature.

Three UI variations were finally pitched, one of which was discarded at the internal testing stage. The Invision prototypes of the remaining two were sent to customers. Both seemed to have an equally adequate reaction, but needed a handful of changes to make certain elements even more evident.

Other Details

Team Size – 1 Designer, 2 Developers
Project Duration – 3 Weeks(1 for Design, 2 for Development & Testing)
Design Areas Touched – Requirement Analysis & Validation, UX, Visual
